The Lineage

Three generations, one bench.

Since 1961 the Marelli family has built by hand what others build by machine — a single body shop, one panel beater's bench passed from father to daughter, and a client book kept in ink, never in software.

Sixty-three years later, the count stands at two hundred and eleven cars. Every one accounted for. Every one still known by name.

The Atelier

Nothing here is stamped.

Every panel is beaten by eye over an English wheel. Every hide is selected, not ordered. Every weave is laid by the same three hands that laid the last two hundred.
